Information:

Here you can find information about one unique example from each type that we have in our aquarium.

Star Fish

Starfish, are remarkable marine creatures. They typically have five arms and come in various shapes and colors. They possess the incredible ability to regenerate lost or damaged arms, and some species can even regenerate into new starfish entirely. Starfish move and capture prey using tube feet, small suction cup-like structures on their arms. They feed on mollusks, small fish, and detritus. Some starfish can reproduce asexually by splitting their body in half, resulting in two separate individuals. These resilient creatures inhabit both shallow and deep waters, showcasing their adaptability.

Bottlenose Dolphin

The bottlenose dolphin is a sociable and intelligent marine mammal known for its distinctive appearance and acrobatic displays. It communicates with clicks and whistles, hunts fish and squid, and is found worldwide.Bottlenose dolphins can reach speeds of up to 20 miles per hour (32 kilometers per hour) when swimming. They can dive to depths of around 1,000 feet (305 meters) and can hold their breath for up to 12 minutes. These dolphins have a lifespan of approximately 40 to 50 years, although some individuals have been known to live longer in captivity.

Manta ray

Manta rays are known for their large size and graceful presence in the ocean. They have a wingspan that can exceed 23 feet (7 meters) and possess unique cephalic fins. These fins help them channel water into their mouths while filter feeding. They are primarily filter feeders, consuming a diet consisting of small fish, plankton, and other microscopic organisms. They have specialized gill rakers that allow them to filter out their prey from the water. These gentle giants are found in tropical and subtropical waters across the globe, including the Indian, Pacific, and Atlantic Oceans.

Cuttlefish

Cuttlefish are known for their camouflaging abilities and unique appearance. With soft bodies and an internal shell called a cuttlebone. These intelligent cephalopods have large, expressive eyes and a mouth surrounded by tentacles. One of the features of cuttlefish is their ability to change color and texture to blend seamlessly with their surroundings. They accomplish this through skin cells called chromatophores. By expanding or contracting these cells, cuttlefish can create intricate patterns and mimic various backgrounds, allowing them to hide from predators or communicate with other individuals.
